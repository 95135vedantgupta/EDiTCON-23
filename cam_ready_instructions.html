Camera-Ready Submission Instructions


Step 1: Formatting your paper


Templates:

Prepare your camera ready manuscript according to the SIG Proceedings Templates at: http://www.acm.org/publications/article-templates/proceedings-template.html. If you have any problems with the templates, please contact ACM's TeX support team directly at Aptara (acmtexsupport@aptaracorp.com).


Page Limits:

  • Regular Papers: 10 pages
  • Concise Papers: 4 pages
  • Workshop Papers: 6 pages

Additional Instructions:

  • The final paper should be PDF with embedded fonts.
  • They should be optimized for fast web viewing.
  • The PDF should not contain page numbers.
  • They should have the rights management statement and bibliographic strip on the bottom of the first page left column (See Step 2).
  • For generating the Computing Classification Code (CCS) for your paper, please see: http://dl.acm.org/ccs/ccs.cfm. After choosing the relevant discipline/subdiscipline, click "Assign this concept" and then choose the level of relevance. Clicking on "view CCS TeX Code" will automatically generate the code block to be placed in your text files.
  • In general, follow the instructions from the authors' guide for SIG proceedings available at: http://www.acm.org/publications/article-templates/sig-alternate-guide.pdf

Step 2: Copyrights Form


ACM will directly contact you via email with information on how to submit a copyright form. This email will come from rightsreview@acm.org (so please ensure that this email address is not caught by your spam filter). You must submit this copyright form first, before submitting your camera-ready manuscript.

After completing the rights management form, authors will be emailed the correct rights management text and bibliographic strip to place within their paper. When the author incorporates the code block generated by the ACM e-Rights system, the correct ACM Rights Statement and bibliographic strip will automatically appear in the author's paper. Please double-check that the ISBN number included correctly, as will be notified here, at a later stage.


Step 3: Submitting your paper


You must submit the camera-ready version of the paper via easychair.org, by accessing the submission page that you have used before

Deadlines for submission of camera-ready papers:
Main Conference: October 15, 2017
Workshops: November 15, 2017
